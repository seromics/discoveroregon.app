A Family Game, 30+ Years in the Making

My Grandma Kathy loved the dice game you've heard of, but she played it differently. She kept a box of little plastic frogs on the table. And whenever you scored early, you'd take out a frog representing your unused roll to save for later. If you were close to getting all five-of-a-kind? We'd all shout "Frog It!" – encouraging you to spend a frog and earn those points!

Today, my family still holds annual tournaments at the lake to crown "The Queen" – the winner receiving our family trophy and bragging rights.

We used to keep those handheld LCD versions of the dice game around our family homes. I've tried other dice game apps, but none were as fun – because what's the fun without Grandma's frogs? Without the frogs, it's just rolling dice and filling categories. The frogs add real strategy and make every game different. I couldn't stand not having a proper version of this for my family when we're not together. So here it is, and I hope you'll love it too.

How It Works

Roll & Score

Roll five dice up to three times per turn. Hold the dice you want and try to complete scoring categories like Three of a Kind, Full House, Large Straight, and more.

Bank Your Frogs

Here's the twist: unused rolls become "frogs" that you save for later. Score early to bank frogs, then spend them when you really need that extra roll.

Go for SPLASH!

Roll all five dice matching for a SPLASH! (Five of a Kind). Your first scores 50 points, and each additional SPLASH earns 100 bonus points.

The Frog Banking Mechanic

Every turn, you get 3 free rolls. But what happens when you score after just 1 or 2 rolls? Those unused rolls become frogs that you bank for later!

Need an extra roll on a crucial turn? Tap "Frog It!" to spend a banked frog and roll again. It's a blend of luck and resource management that transforms the classic dice game.

Ways to Play

Solo Play

Practice your strategy and aim for high scores. Games with standard rules are eligible for trophies.

Local Multiplayer

Pass the device and compete with 2-4 players. Each player has their own scorecard and frog bank.

Online Challenges

Weekly tournaments and daily wildcards with special rules. Submit your best to the global leaderboard.
